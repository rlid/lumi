What is LumiAsk?

LumiAsk started as a tool that helps you track and gain traction on the questions you ask in online communities. If you are not having much luck with your message on a Discord server, or your post / comment on Reddit or other social media platforms, you are welcome to give LumiAsk a go and see how much further some extra incentive will take you.

How does it work?

If you have a question to ask...
  1. Set a topic (e.g. something you are interested in) and set a reward to participants involved in getting your question successfully answered
  2. Chat with anyone interested to help you and provide clarifications if needed
  3. Accept a request for engagement when you are ready, remember to check the answerer's credentials if necessary
  4. Ask your question and rate the engagement after an answer is provided
If you want to help with an existing post...
  1. Chat with the OP (Original Poster) for any clarifications needed
  2. Request for engagement when you are ready, or share the post with anyone who might be able to help. If you are sharing, make sure to use your unique referrer link using the "Share" button
  3. After the request is accepted, help the OP and rate the engagement when you are done
(New!) If you are posting as an answerer...
  1. Set a topic (something you are knowledgeable about) and set a reward that you want to receive after successfully answering a question
  2. Chat with anyone interested in the topic and provide clarifications if needed
  3. Accept any request for engagement when you are ready
  4. Answer the questions and rate the engagement when you are done

If both the asker and the answerer rate the engagement as successful, a reward will be distributed from the asker to the answerer and all referrers leading to the successful engagement.

What is private mode?

If you post in private mode, your post will not be listed publicly on the platform, and it can only be accessed with a link from you or the referrers. This feature relies on you to send the link to your friends or an online community to kick off the process.

With private mode, we hope that we can offer extra incentives for referrers to spread the word without worrying about not getting recognised when rewards are distributed.

You should use private mode if your question is easily answered by the right person (e.g. if you are looking for someone to share a specific experience), but might require a search beyond your immediate network of friends and active members of online communities.

What happens after my post is submitted?

In public mode, you can just wait for other users to take interest in your post and contact you or refer your post. We also use our own channels to connect you with someone to help you. You can also ask your friends to spread the word.

In private mode, nothing happens until you kick off the process by sending a link to the post to a Discord server, post it on Reddit or other social media platforms.

Why do my friends see a different reward from what I set for my post?

The reward displayed is dependent on the viewer. When another user views your post, they will see how much they would receive / pay on posts where you are the asker / answerer. If they are viewing your post through a referrer's link, the referrer's reward will be taken into account.

For example, if you offer $5 on a question you are asking, other viewers will see $4.5 as the reward because that is the amount they would receive by answering the question, after the platform fee. If a user sends the post to a friend using a referral link, their friend would see $4 as the reward after the platform fee and the referrer's reward.

If you are logged in, you should always see the rewards you set on your posts.

What if I receive multiple requests for engagements on the same post?

You can accept multiple active engagements at the same time, e.g. you want to hear from different perspectives / a second opinion to cross-check the answers, but as an asker you should be prepared to pay the reward for each successful engagement when you accept.

If you only need one person to help you, feel free to pick the most suitable one based on your criteria and ignore the others.

What happens if an engagement is rated differently by the asker and the answerer?

An engagement can be rated as successful(+) or unsuccessful(−) by the asker and the answerer. No reward is distributed if there is any discrepancy in the asker and answerer's rating of the engagement.

A dispute arises if the asker rates − and the answerer rates +, the party with a weaker trustworthiness on the platform will have its trustworthiness negatively impacted

There will be no dispute as long as the answerer rates the engagement as −, in which case the trustworthiness of neither party will be directly impacted

If the askers can always decide not pay the reward by giving a negative rating, do I have any protection as the answerer?

If a buyer does not pay a reward by giving a negative rating untruthfully, their trustworthiness will be aggressively reduced very quickly, and they will lose the ability to make new post or enter into engagements.

All the information about past engagements are available on a user's public profile, so if you are not comfortable with the user's track record, you can choose not to engage with such user even before their account becomes restricted due to a low trustworthiness score.

When is payment taken?

Payment is not required at the time of posting.

As the asker, before you can accept an engagement from a prospective answerer or request for engagement on an answerer's post, your balance will be checked and a top-up will be required if your balance is not enough for the reward.

Once an engagement is entered into, an amount equal to the total reward is reserved in the seller's account. If the engagement is rated as successful by both the asker and the answerer, the reserved amount will be distributed to the participants involved. Otherwise, the reserved amount will be released back to the asker's account.

How do you ensure the quality of the answers provided?

We are constantly releasing new features for the purpose of promoting responsible behavior, maintaining and improving the quality user-generated contents, and helping users to assess the trustworthiness of others. Our current recommendations are as follows:

  1. Ask questions that you will know if an answer is satisfactory when given, e.g. because the answer can be verified by yourself once enough proof is presented by the answerer (e.g. you ask for a coupon code and the one given by the answerer works), or because the answerer convinced you of their credentials through proof (e.g. you want to know about a university and the answerer showed you his ID with sensitive information redacted), or because you are prepared to enter into multiple engagements to cross-check the answers.
  2. Make sure to check out the other user's track record of past engagements before entering into an engagement. While this does not prevent someone from giving poor answers, it should deter people from entering into engagements when they are not sure if they can help.
  3. There is a limit on the reward of posts a user can participate in, which is reduced aggressively for users on a bad run of engagements. Such users cannot participate in high reward posts, and you will be warned if their limit is reduced after they entered into engagement with you.
  4. In addition to rating the engagement, please report to us particularly poor interactions or deliberate misinformation.

Developing effective strategies to maintain and improve the quality of user-generated contents and holding the sources accountable is one of the main motivations for us to create the platform, and it will always remain an area of active research and development for us. How are the rewards distributed?

Following a successful engagement, the reward is distributed to all participants in the chain leading to the successful engagement.

  • In public mode:

    The total referral reward is capped at 20% of the post reward set by the OP.

    The last referrer (who directly introduced the answerer) gets half of the total referral reward (i.e. 10%), and each earlier referrer gets half of the remaining referral reward (e.g. the second last referrer gets 5%, the third last referrer gets 2.5%, and so on).

    The answerer gets all the remaining reward after a platform fee of 10%, i.e. between 90% (no referrers) and ~70% of the post reward.

  • In private mode:

    The total referral reward is capped at 40% of the post reward set by the OP.

    Each referrer sets their own share of the referral reward to claim, the default being half of the remaining referral reward, e.g. the first referrer (closest to the OP) in a chain gets 20% by default.

    The answerer gets all the remaining reward after a platform fee of 10%, i.e. between 90% (no referrers) and ~50% of the post reward.

How much can I earn from referrals?

In public mode, you can earn up to 10% of the post reward set by the OP, if your referral leads to a successful engagement directly. You can still earn a smaller reward if your participation is in a chain of referrals that leads to a successful engagement.

In private mode, it can be as much as 40% of the post reward set by the OP.

I did not get my referrer's reward I was expecting - why?

Make sure you are sharing your own referrer's link by using the "Share" button. Simply sending a link to the post will not properly record your contribution.

Does it really work?

You should probably always start with a simpler way, e.g. a Google search, asking friends or an online community, and there is no reason to use this platform if you can get the answer more easily. But there are times when your question is so specific that no one else seems to care without extra incentive, and you want something that reaches a little deeper than your own social circle, or go beyond the active users of a community.

How to ask a question that is not answered here?
